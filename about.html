About the creation of the website, the inspiration

This website was created as an educational project to explore stellar evolution and the classification of stars. The goal of the site is to provide a visual and interactive way to understand how stars form, evolve, and eventually reach the final stages of their lives.

Other Simulation Websites

Some well-known examples include the NASA Eyes visualization tools, the PhET Interactive Simulations developed at the University of Colorado Boulder, and educational astronomy tools used in university courses. These resources inspired the design of the simulation section of this website.

Examples include: Stellar Evolution Timeline Simulation and Star in a Box .

Why These Simulations Are Useful

Stellar evolution is difficult to visualize because it occurs over extremely long timescales and involves complex physical processes. Interactive simulations help bridge this gap by allowing users to observe how changes in mass affect a star’s temperature, luminosity, and lifetime in real time.

Crab Nebula

Credit: NASA Gallery

The Crab Nebula is a famous supernova remnant located about 6,500 light-years from Earth in the constellation Taurus. It was created when a massive star exploded in a supernova observed by astronomers in the year 1054.

Current Research

Modern astronomy continues to study stellar evolution using both ground-based observatories and space telescopes. Instruments such as the Hubble Space Telescope and the James Webb Space Telescope allow astronomers to observe star-forming regions, supernova remnants, and distant stellar populations with unprecedented detail.
